Let's Make TV Ã—

Let's Make TV

  • Duration: 2 Hours
  • Schedule: April 14, Saturday, 14:15

In this workshop the participants are confronted with the proposal of a television news program, live, without stops, as in television.

Participants begin by attending a short theoretical introduction to the specific grammar of audiovisuals. Topics such as plans, frames, concepts of image, rule of thirds and third of image, camera movements, camera angulation and height, multi-camera work, studio interview and outdoor interview, etc. are discussed, an approach that lasts no more than half an hour.

Afterwards the participants are distributed by the functions they will occupy in the Newscast. (Editor, anchors, journalists, meteorologist, studio and street reporters, studio and overseas reporting camera operators, VT operators, video and audio mixer operators, graphic designers and assistants).

Each one is explained its function and, if appropriate, explained how the equipment with which it will work, works.

The necessary tests are performed (usually only 1 or 2) and the recording starts. At the end you see the work done.
